Indian Wicket Keeper Batsman: MS Dhoni

MS Dhoni is the most successful Indian captain ever. From the ICC Champions Trophy to the mega ICC Cricket World Cup, MS Dhoni has achieved everything a captain can wish for.

Profile

  • Full name : Mahendra Singh Dhoni
  • Born : 7 July 1981 (age 35)
    Ranchi, Bihar, India (Now Jharkhand)
  • Role : Wicket-keeper, Indian Captain
  • Test debut (cap 251) : 2 December 2005 v Sri Lanka
  • Last Test : 26 December 2014 v Australia
  • ODI debut (cap 158) : 23 December 2004 v Bangladesh
  • T20I debut (cap 2) : 1 December 2006 v South Africa

Dhoni holds numerous captaincy records such as most wins by an Indian captain in Tests and ODIs, and most back-to-back wins by an Indian captain in ODIs. He took over the ODI captaincy from Rahul Dravid in 2007 and led the team to its first-ever bilateral ODI series wins in Sri Lanka and New Zealand. Little did the world know that when Mahendra Singh Dhoni walked out of Melbourne Cricket Ground (MCG) after helping India secure a draw against Australia, it would be the last time he would be doing so in Test cricket.

Dhoni�s decision to retire from Test cricket shocked the cricket fraternity and the Twitter space was flooded with tributes pouring from all over the world.

Dhoni, who made his debut in December 2005, played 90 Tests, leading 60 of them. Sachin Tendulkar, who retired from cricket a year back, congratulated Dhoni on his marvellous career. The entire cricket world paid rich tributes to the Indian skipper, who undoubtedly is among the modern greats of the game.
